## Overview

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) has revolutionized the study of complex biological systems by enabling transcriptomic profiling at single-cell resolution and uncovering cellular heterogeneity that is obscured in bulk sequencing approaches.

This project presents a comprehensive atlas-scale benchmarking framework designed to systematically evaluate both the independent and coupled effects of:

- Batch integration methods
- Trajectory inference methods

within large multi-sample liver cancer scRNA-seq datasets.

To support this evaluation, a comprehensive pan-liver cancer single-cell atlas was constructed by integrating nine publicly available datasets spanning hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), cholangiocarcinoma (CCA), colorectal liver metastases (CRLM), tumor microenvironments, and healthy liver tissue.

The resulting atlas contains:

- **327,733 cells pre-QC**
- **304,620 cells post-QC**
- **106 patients/donors**
- **9 integrated public datasets**
This study includes:

- **11 integration methods**
- **8 trajectory inference methods**
- **14 quantitative integration metrics**
- **6 curated lineage differentiation tasks**
- **147 total benchmarking experiments**

The framework systematically investigates how upstream batch correction strategies reshape latent space topology and influence downstream trajectory reconstruction, pseudotime ordering, lineage branching structure, and biological interpretability.

# Benchmarking Framework

## Level I — Independent Benchmarking

### Integration Benchmarking

Evaluation of 11 state-of-the-art integration methods using 14 quantitative metrics assessing:

- Batch mixing performance
- Biological conservation
- Cell type preservation
- Local and global structure retention

### Trajectory Inference Benchmarking

Evaluation of 8 trajectory inference methods across 6 curated lineage differentiation tasks representing diverse biological processes and differentiation programs.

---

## Level II — Coupled Benchmarking

To investigate the interaction between integration and trajectory inference:

- 4 trajectory inference methods were applied to
- Outputs generated by all 11 integration methods
- Across 2 global lineage tasks

yielding:

- **88 coupled experiments**

This analysis reveals how integration strategies alter latent space geometry and downstream trajectory reconstruction.

# Key Findings

- Integration methods substantially reshape latent space topology and neighborhood structure.
- Improved batch mixing does not necessarily preserve biologically meaningful trajectories.
- Different integration strategies can significantly alter pseudotime ordering and lineage branching.
- Coupled benchmarking reveals strong interactions between integration and trajectory inference methods.
- Real atlas-scale biological datasets expose challenges not captured by simulated benchmarking frameworks.
